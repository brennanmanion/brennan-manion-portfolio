Trading

I swing trade stock options. In order to take a trade, I must first have an understanding of how the broad market is performing, how each sector is performing, and how stocks in a sector are performing. Then, I look for a burden of proof to take a trade. I execute the trade, set a stop loss and take profit, and follow my trading plan strictly.

Beginning each morning, I take a look at market breadth, fear levels, the put to call ratio, support and resistance levels of the SPY, the IWM, and the TLT, and which sectors are being accumulated. I then look at the performance of each of the 10 major sectors to understand which sectors are performing best. I then identify which are the top performing stocks in that sector. Following these strategies will give me a direction of where the overall market is going and which sectors will lead or lag the market movement. After understanding the forest, I then take a look at the trees.

After having a broad market understanding, I can begin charting the best performing stocks in the best performing sector. I chart the stock to find congestion patterns, support and resistance levels, symmetry in price swings, volume patterns, and momentum and price action divergence. In order to take a trade, I must have at least 3 of these factors in confluence with market and sector bullishness. The best trade setups I have are stocks that are top performers in a strong market and sector environment, that have pulled back to a support level with a distance similar to historical price action swings, with divergence between price action and the relative strength index, and volume to confirm the trend. I will then set my take profit and stop loss levels, and long a call that has liquidity, a tight spread, and a high delta with 10 weeks minimum to expiration.

After finding a good trade set up, I set notifications on my thinkorswim app on my phone to identify when the mark is at my take profit or stop loss levels. I will additionally make note of when I placed the trade. If the stock moves to my desired profit target, I will liquidate my option contract. If the stock moves below my stop loss level, I will liquidate the option contract. If 15 trading sessions have expired and no movement has occurred in the stock, I will liquidate my contract. Finally, if a volatile move occurs (upside or downside) I will liquidate my contract. I follow these rules with great discipline.

I will only take a trade when the odds are in my favor. I will not take a position unless a great set up is presented to me, and the market pulls back to the level I consider fair value. I follow my stop loss and take profit levels strictly, and will only move my stop loss level up when a new high is made. I give myself exposure to long and short positions to create natural hedges for my portfolio. I follow strict position sizing rules, and seek to make steady gains through trading over my lifetime.
